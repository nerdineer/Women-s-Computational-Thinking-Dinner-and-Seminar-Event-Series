Meet Our Mentors!

  At each event you will have a mentor working with you and discussing the event topics. All mentors are currently undergraduate students here at Oregon State. Feel free to learn more about each of us as well as contact us if you have any questions relating to, attending OSU or our general college experience.

Claudia

I first got interested in CS when I TAed a computer science course in high school. In this course, I got to see a variety of projects being made as well as discovered that CS was applicable to many different fields. I now apply psychology to CS, to help create better user interfaces and improve the user's exerpience. CS has taught me how to think differently about problems I approach and has also helped me learn how to break down problems further, rather than look at them from a big picture. When I am not working on school, research or TAing for the intro CS courses, I like to spend time with my friends and family, watch movies, and read.

Melissa

I got interested in CS/ECE when I realized I love to play video games and figuring out how things work. Studying this subject has made me look at the world in a completely different way, for example, I can now explain how something was made rather than guessing. It has helped me with every day challenges, such as being able to come up with different approaches to unexpected problems. Studying this subject is not the only thing I do, I love to ski, ride horses, run, and hang out with my friends.

Katie

I started computer science when I was in high school; I needed another class and figured I liked computers and I liked science, so I should try this "computer science" thing. I loved the creative problem solving and the logic involved and fell in love with computer science. CS has made me approach problems differently and because of it, I know how to decompose large tasks into small things I can take care of myself without being overwhelmed. Throughout high school and college, I have also been highly involved with my school's color guard and marching band!
